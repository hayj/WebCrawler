7 principles for using speed as a competitive advantage

Sep 15, 2015, 11:19am EDT

Startups

Learning how to use speed as a competitive advantage is imperative today. Here are some principles that can help you succeed over the competition:

1. Be willing to try and fail rapidly

Ive been a speaker about technology for many years. As I speak to executives and other decision makers around the world, Ive seen many times that those who can fail fast do better. Ive found that I usually have to try a new technology a few sometimes several times before I finally get it.

Those who succeed are willing to pay the price to achieve success. Dont do something that creates failure on a massive, harmful-to-people scale. Plan your experiments in learning so that if you fail, which you will, the costs are minimized and controlled. Learn from those failures and setbacks. They are often your best teacher. After failing a few times and learning each time you will be able to succeed where others dont.

2. Know how to use your go-to tools

It will take some time, money and energy to learn how to use the right tools. However, this will pay off very well for you when the time comes. Take time to learn how to use the right tools. Get the help you need.

3. Do it now

W. Clement Stone was a great success in the area of insurance and personal development. He would use the phrase, Do it now to encourage those around him to stop fiddling around and take results-generating action. When youre in that mode of wondering if you should do something, use his phrase to motivate yourself and get the work done.

4. Continually train to be ready

Learn from special forces and combat aircraft fighters. They are constantly training and learning. This is the majority of their time. They train continuously so that when the emergency signal comes, and they are needed rapidly in an urgent situation, they are ready. Be professional. Always be ready to swing into action when needed.

5. Shorten your production and your time-to-market

This gives you a strong advantage. Leverage technologies like 3D printing, which can prototype quickly. Leverage the daylights out of technologies that prove to give you an advantage. While the competition is sleeping or complaining about some new technology, you will want to keep experimenting and learning from various small failures and setbacks.

6. Keep your systems in place

Have your checklists ready to go. Develop the systems that work for you. Keep refining them. This will help you to operate from a system that automatically works, and you dont have to create and test for something new in the moment of crisis.

7. Think shorter life cycles along with faster and cheaper production technologies

Have a quick and responsive supply chain that can adapt quickly to market changes. This comes way before the emergency hits. Remember the saying, Dig your well before you are thirsty. Plan for rapid deployment before you need it.

These seven steps can help you and your organization to be ready very fast. Speed-to-market and speed-to-readiness help you gain a competitive advantage. What other tools and procedures have helped you to achieve a level of fast implementation? I look forward to hearing from you.
